Punjab, a state in northern India, is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and agricultural prosperity. Often called the "Land of Five Rivers," Punjab has been a significant region since ancient times, shaping India's history through the Indus Valley Civilization, Sikh heritage, and the struggles for independence. The Punjabi language and its lively traditions, including Bhangra and Gidda dance forms, reflect the state's dynamic spirit. The state has been home to great warriors, poets, and freedom fighters, making it an important center of India’s cultural and historical identity. Punjab is renowned for its magnificent gurdwaras, with the Golden Temple in Amritsar being the holiest shrine of Sikhism and a major pilgrimage site. The historic Jallianwala Bagh, Wagah Border ceremony, and Anandpur Sahib add to the state's historical significance. Apart from religious and historical sites, Punjab’s bustling cities like Ludhiana, Jalandhar, and Chandigarh offer a mix of modernity and tradition. The lush green fields of wheat and mustard, along with the sight of tractors plowing the land, highlight Punjab’s status as the "Granary of India." ... Punjabi cuisine is world-famous for its rich flavors and hearty meals. Signature dishes such as butter chicken, sarson da saag with makki di roti, chole bhature, and Amritsari kulcha are loved by food enthusiasts. The traditional lassi, a refreshing yogurt-based drink, is a staple in Punjabi households. The warmth and hospitality of Punjab’s people, combined with its energetic festivals like Lohri, Baisakhi, and Gurpurab, make the state a land of joy and celebration.

Attractions

Explore the place of piety and natural beauty

 

Golden temple

Golden Temple in Amritsar is a sacred Sikh shrine known for its stunning golden architecture, spiritual significance, and peaceful surroundings.

 

Gurdwara Fatehgarh, Sahib

Gurdwara Fatehgarh Sahib in Punjab is a historic Sikh shrine commemorating the martyrdom of Guru Gobind Singh’s younger sons.

 

Derawar Fort

Derawar Fort in Punjab is a massive square fortress in the Cholistan Desert, known for its towering bastions and historic significance.

 

Shahi Hammam

Shahi Hammam in Lahore is a beautifully preserved Mughal-era bathhouse known for its stunning frescoes, Persian-style architecture, and historical significance.

 

Sheikhupura Fort

Sheikhupura Fort in Pakistan is a historic Mughal-era fortress known for its grand architecture and connection to Emperor Jahangir.

 

Sri Harmandir Sahib

Sri Harmandir Sahib, also known as the Golden Temple, is a sacred Sikh shrine in Amritsar, renowned for its golden architecture.
