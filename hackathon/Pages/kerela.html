Kerala was formed on 1st November 1956 as a state of India, following the States Reorganisation Act. Located on the southwestern coast of the country, it is bordered by Karnataka to the north, Tamil Nadu to the east and south, and the Arabian Sea to the west. Known for its lush greenery, serene backwaters, and picturesque landscapes, Kerala is rich in natural resources, including rivers, forests, and diverse wildlife. It is famous for its cultural heritage, classical dance forms like Kathakali, and Ayurvedic traditions. Often called "God’s Own Country," Kerala is a major tourist destination with attractions like Munnar, Alleppey, Wayanad, and Kovalam. Thiruvananthapuram is the capital of Kerala, known for its historic landmarks and scenic coastline. The state has the highest literacy rate in India and is renowned for its social development and quality of life. ... A land of enchanting backwaters, lush greenery, serene beaches, and vibrant culture, Kerala is a paradise for travelers. Popularly known as "God’s Own Country," the state is nestled between the Arabian Sea and the Western Ghats, offering breathtaking landscapes and diverse experiences. Kerala is famous for its houseboat cruises in Alleppey, misty hills of Munnar, wildlife in Periyar, and pristine beaches like Varkala and Kovalam. The state is also a spiritual and wellness hub, known for its ancient Ayurvedic treatments and yoga retreats. With a rich heritage reflected in its temples, festivals, and classical arts like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, Kerala promises a perfect blend of nature, culture, and relaxation for every traveler.

Pilgrimage

Journey through a realm of spirituality and serenity

 

Munroe Island

Munroe Island in Kerala is a scenic backwater destination known for its serene canals, lush greenery, and traditional village life.

 

Munnar

Munnar, a famous hill station in Kerala, is renowned for its tea plantations, misty hills, cool climate, and breathtaking landscapes.

 

Alleppey houseboat

Alleppey houseboats in Kerala offer a serene backwater experience with luxurious stays, scenic views, and traditional cuisine.

 

Kathakali

Kathakali is a classical Kerala dance-drama known for its elaborate costumes, expressive gestures, and intricate makeup.

 

Athirappilly water falls

Athirappilly Waterfalls in Kerala is a majestic cascade, often called the "Niagara of India," surrounded by lush greenery.

 

Varkala

Varkala in Kerala is a scenic coastal town known for its stunning cliffs, serene beaches, and the sacred Janardanaswamy Temple.
