Uttar Pradesh, the fourth largest and most populous state of India, is located in the northern part of the country. It shares international borders with Nepal in the north and is surrounded by Uttarakhand and Himachal Pradesh in the northwest, Haryana, Delhi, and Rajasthan in the west, Madhya Pradesh in the south, and Bihar and Jharkhand in the east. The state is rich in historical and cultural heritage, home to iconic landmarks like the Taj Mahal, Varanasi, and Ayodhya. The Ganges, Yamuna, and their tributaries flow through its fertile plains, making it an agricultural hub. Lucknow, the capital city, is known for its Mughal and Nawabi influences, exquisite cuisine, and vibrant arts. Uttar Pradesh is also a major center for religious tourism, with sacred sites like Kashi Vishwanath, Mathura, and Prayagraj drawing millions of pilgrims every year.... Uttar Pradesh, the heart of India, is known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and political significance. It is home to renowned institutions like BHU and AMU and famous for crafts like Lucknow’s Chikankari and Varanasi’s silk. The state hosts grand festivals such as the Kumbh Mela and Taj Mahotsav, blending tradition with modernity.
